How to Apply

Requests for support from the Foundation are made at the Foundation website www.ZOLLfoundation.org at any time via a simple application available on the site. Applications are reviewed periodically by the Board and the Foundation will acknowledge receipt of all applications. Approvals by the Board of Directors are oriented toward:

  • early research in new therapies or techniques;
  • reviews of data and information to better understand practices in the field of resuscitation and acute critical care; and
  • educational activities combined with measurement to enhance care.
  • promising projects where initial results may allow researchers to seek additional funding from other sources for continued research that will require more extensive trials or scale.

The Foundation attempts to respond to all proposals in six months or less and will on occasion request further information or have direct discussions with grant applicants. For research Grants the applicant must have a reference from another health care professional recommending the project and the individual applying for the grant. Grant applicants must also disclose any conflicts of interest in accordance with their institutions or agencies policies in their grant application

Approved grants and approved requests for other funding will be listed on the Foundation Website.

Report of Activities Associated with Grants

Grant recipients are required to provide a report on the activity for which funding is received whether the activity is a research activity or a supported event or program that is not related to research. A simple form for these reports is available on the website.

Grant Eligibility

Grant applicants must meet minimum criteria:

  • Have a formal association with an institution or agency involved in resuscitation or acute critical care in a medical, scientific, educational or administrative capacity.
  • Is an agency institution or association involved in resuscitation or acute critical care?
  • If an individual is an applicant they must not be under any professional restrictions and have all required certifications or licenses necessary to practice their occupation.
